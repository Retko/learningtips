Concentration Tips

Choose what works for you.
  • Take one thing at a time.
  • Turn off everything distracting if possible.
  • Turn off TV.
  • If you like to listen some music, it’s better not to listen music with lyrics.
  • If you have to stop learning, write down where you finished.
  • Work on you concentration. It’s not one time thing, creating a habit take some time.
  • Take it by small steps. Try to concentrate for 10 minutes at the beginning. Then add 5 minutes and so on.
  • Try different times of the day for learning. For some people it’s better in the morning or in the night etc.
  • Reciting aloud can help.
  • Think about why you learn and good job that you can get.
  • Inversely you can think about bad jobs that you would have go to if you won’t learn.
  • Plan your free time and tell yourself that you will try to concentrate completely until then.
  • Exercise. Don’t have to go to fitness, 10-20 minutes can sometimes be very good for your concentration.
  • You can try binaural beats and other similar tones or music. Youtube is full of thins things.
  • Nature sounds are good for some people to improve concentration.
  • Try to use highlighters.
  • Drink tea. Green is good for concentration.
  • Clean your room.
  • Use short notes, but write them yourself.
  • Clear your mind. Try to put stress away, solve your problems. This can be long term.
  • Try Keyword Technique. Choose a word of your subject, and when you lose concentration repeat the word several times.
  • Being in the NOW -- the present moment. Not thinking about the past or the future. Just the now.
  • 5 more rule. From now on, if you're in the middle of a task and tempted to give up -- just do FIVE MORE. Read FIVE MORE pages. Finish FIVE MORE math problems. Work FIVE MORE minutes.
  • Whenever doing important work, use a concentration scoresheet. Each time you find your mind wandering, make a check mark on the sheet.
  • Break task into smaller parts.
